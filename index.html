Coronaviruses : These RNA viruses, including the COVID-19 causative agent, are known for their ability to cause respiratory illnesses and have posed significant global health challenges in recent years. Dengue Virus : A mosquito-borne RNA virus that can lead to a range of symptoms, from mild fever to life-threatening complications, and is a major public health concern in tropical and subtropical regions. Avian Influenza (Bird Flu) Virus : These RNA viruses primarily infect birds but can also cross the species barrier and cause severe illness in humans, posing a potential pandemic threat. Lassa Virus : A zoonotic RNA virus that can cause Lassa fever, a potentially fatal viral hemorrhagic fever found primarily in West Africa. Ebola Virus : A highly contagious and deadly RNA virus that can cause severe and often fatal viral hemorrhagic fever, primarily in Africa. Marburg Virus : Another RNA virus that can cause a rare and severe viral hemorrhagic fever, similar to Ebola, and has the potential for outbreaks with high mortality rates.
